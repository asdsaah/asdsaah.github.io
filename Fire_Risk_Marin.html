Modeling Fire Risk in Marin County, CA

Title: Modeling Fire Risk in Marin County.
Authors: Andrew Saah, Ellis Richburg, Giancarlo Laguna
Abstract: This study presents a wildfire risk model for Marin County, California, using remotely sensed data and geospatial analysis. We integrate vegetation health (NDVI, NDWI), topography (slope, aspect), fuel load (Andersonâ€™s Fuel Models), and ignition sources (roads, power lines, recreation sites, WUI areas) to estimate spatial patterns of fire likelihood. By classifying and combining these factors within a GIS environment, we produce a comprehensive risk surface highlighting areas prone to fire. Comparison with historical burn perimeters suggests that the model effectively identifies higher-risk zones, serving as a useful decision support tool for management, planning, and mitigation strategies aimed at reducing wildfire impacts.
